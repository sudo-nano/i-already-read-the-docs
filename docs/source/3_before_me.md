# 3 Before Me: Tech Edition

## 3 Before Me in Education
**3 Before Me** is an educational strategy that instructs students to consult 3
sources before asking their teacher. 

1. **Ask yourself:** Think about the question and about whether you already have the knowledge to answer it.
2. **Check course materials:** Look at the syllabus, schedule, and textbook for any relevant information.
3. **Ask a friend or classmate:** Ask someone who isn't your teacher for help. 

This is useful both as a skill in its own right, and as a way to decrease load
on the teacher. 

## 3 Before Me in Tech
Outside of an education environment, instead of a teacher, you have some other
authoritative person or group on a topic. That could be your boss, it could be
maintainers on an open source project. Often, the actual authoritative people
are extremely limited in their time, and will only respond to forum posts or
git forge issues that have already been triaged by the community. In these
cases, I consider the community to also be an authoritative source, so you
should consult your 3 sources before opening a new forum thread or issue report.

### Ask Yourself
When it comes to troubleshooting tech problems, asking yourself is undoubtedly
the most important step. It comes with a fun sub-step: ask yourself what the
problem actually is, not what your attempted solution is. Considering the root
problem rather than the solution you're currently attempting will help you find
solutions and troubleshooting steps you might have otherwise written off. 
For more on this, see [xyproblem.info](https://xyproblem.info/). 

After you know what the root problem is, consider whether it can be solved with
any tools you're familiar with. If not, consider what tools you know, but may
not be familiar with, that could solve the problem.
